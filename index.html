What to Include in Your Listing for Maximum Results

Video: Video is the single most important element of any online horse listing. Buyers want to see the horse move, jump, and go on the flat before arranging a trial. Include at least one quality under saddle video and ideally a jump video at the horse's current working height.

Photos: High-quality conformation photos and action shots showing the horse's jump style, movement, and overall impression. Good photos dramatically increase listing click-through rates and buyer inquiries.

Show Record: Document the horse's competition history — shows attended, divisions competed, championships and ribbons won. Buyers searching for horses with specific qualifications (Pony Finals qualified, IHD enrolled, NAL Finals record) filter specifically for documented show records.

Accurate Description: Height, age, breed, current working level, suitability, price or price range, and lease options if applicable. Be honest and specific — buyers who receive accurate information are more likely to become serious inquiries.
